Studies and Projects

[2020] Optimized intervention against Covid-19 in a complex network

COMORBUSS is a computational model for the spread of diseases by the general social dynamics of any community. The fundamental goal of any containment measure for a disease like SARS-COVID-19 is to suppress (and ultimately break) the chain of contacts that sustains viral spread. Unfortunately, the same measures taken in different communities often do not have the same effectiveness due to different population densities, service structures, and social behavior. Therefore, while extreme containment policies may be necessary in some cities, the same results can be achieved through less restrictive and harmful measures in smaller communities.

Statistical Modelling Differential Equations Optimization

[2022] Detection of changes in parameters of diffusion processes for optimal execution strategies

Proposed ways of detecting changes in parameters of financial time series being modelled by diffusion processes.

Time Series Analysis Stochastic Modelling

[2021] How User Information Drives Amazon's Products Recommendations

Scraped information about more than 60,000 Amazon's products by mimicking the behaviour/interests of seven different personas (sports, shopping, science, home, health, games and computers) through the usage of cookies and HTTP request parameters. Proceded, then, to analyse whether there is evidence of price discrimination or price steering between each persona's recommended products. Later on, analysed the importance of each seller's features on Amazon's seller recommendation on the product's buy-box.

Data Scraping Cookies Manipulation Statistical Analysis

[2021] Generative models for financial losses

Proposed a generative model to produce synthetic data for losses in the stock market.

Time Series Analysis Generative Models

[2021] Effects of the Interest Rate on the Brazilian Stock Market

The impacts of the interest rate on eight distinct sectors of the Brazilian stock market were examined using financial indices as proxies for each sector. The research was conducted by applying both a time series (SARIMAX) and a linear regression model.

Time Series Analysis

[2021] Analysis of economic incentives of fake reviews for Parisian restaurants on Yelp

Scraped information about more than 250,000 reviews from over 13,000 parisian restaurants on the Yelp social platform and proceeded to analyse the effects of restaurant competitiveness on review fraud, both for self improvement and for harming competitors. Later, the polarization difference between fake and genuine reviews, as well as the characteristics of users who publish fraudulent reviews, were examined.
